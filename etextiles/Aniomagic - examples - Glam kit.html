Glam is an ultra simple etextiles kit, made especially for beginners, no computer required.

Change light patterns by pressing the touch pad. Use it by itself, or with 2 LED lights.

Glam comes already programmed.

This basic tutorial shows how the pieces fit together. Here, we use 2 plain LEDS, some felt, and a battery holder made from adhesive dots.



The Glam kit contains:
  • Glam
  • 2 led boards
  • 3 yards conductive thread
  • thin battery (cr2016)
  • 3 adhesive-backed liners

  you'll also need:
  • some cloth or felt
  • a needle
  • a pair of scissors
  • scotch tape (optional, to hold thread)
  • fabric glue (optional, to seal knots)
 
  Click on the pictures to see the larger versions.


The very first time you use Glam, it helps to align all the pieces in a row, so the wiring is straight without any cross over.

Later on, you can move things around to make more elaborate designs.

Arrange the LEDs around Glam so that they have their + side up.

This is what they would look like from underneath.

Notice Glam and the two LEDs have their + side up.

Cut an 8" piece of thread and tie a knot at one end, then thread into a needle.

Pass the needle from the back side off the cloth through the top + hole on Glam.

The + side is slightly bigger than the other petals.

Pull the thread all the way through...

... until you hit the knot on the back.

If the knot goes through your cloth, try again, this time pull gently until the knot barely touches the back of the cloth.

Pass the thread on the outside of the hole on Glam...

... and through the cloth.

To make a good, solid connection, it's important to pass the thread 3-4 times from the back of the cloth, up through the hole, over, and back down through the cloth.

Leave 3 inches of thread, and cut.

This will be used to coil into the battery holder.

Do the same thing for the bottom - hole on Glam.

From the back of the cloth, through the hole, over, and back through the cloth. Repeat 3-4 times.

Leave about 3 inches extra before cutting the thread.



Hold the top thread (connected to the + hole of Glam) to the + of the battery, then hold the bottom thread to the other side of the battery.

Glam should light up and start flashing.

If it doesn't light up, make sure

  • the top thread is not touching the bottom thread
  • the bottom thread is not touching any part of the top of the battery
  • wiggle Glam and see if the connection improves

This step must work before continuing, so double check your connections

Tape down the + and - thread out of the way as you sew on the other parts.



Each LED board has a 100 ohm resistor built in, and you can mix orange, yellow, and green. You can also use white and blue LEDs but they won't be as bright.

Glam itself has orange, yellow, or green LEDs only.

This is what it looks like underneath.

Align LED + side up, to match Glam.

Connect the top right hole on Glam to the top hole on the LEDS.

Go through each hole 3-4 times for a good, solid connection.

When you go through 4 times, tie and knot and cut the thread.

Now connect from the lower right hole on Glam to the bottom hole on the LED.

Look for any stray thread from one hole that might be touching another hole. You can trim, use tape, or fabric glue to prevent short circuits.

Do the same with the other LED.

When you've sewn the 2 LEDs, the back of the cloth should look like this

Connect Glam to the battery as before to make sure all the lights come on.



Use the adhesive-backed liner to protect the conductive thread and connections.

Peel off the backing and pass the top (+) thread through the top hole, and the bottom (-) thread through the middle hole.

Pull the threads taut so the there's no slack, and the place the liner, adhesive-side down.

The battery holder consists of two round discs.

One has adhesive backing on one side.

The other has a hole in the middle with adhesive backing on both sides.

Peel off the adhesive backing on one side of the disc.

Pass the bottom - thread through the hole in the disc

Place the disc, sticky side down, unto the cloth while pulling the thread all the way through.

Remove the top adhesive backing.

Wind the - thread into a coil. This is the - part of the battery holder.

Press the battery, + side up, unto the coil.

Peel off the adhesive backing off the second disc.

Wind the + thread into a coil. This is the + part of the battery holder.

Press the + disc unto the battery.

And that's how we Glam up!
